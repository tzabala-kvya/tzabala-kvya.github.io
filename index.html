About

Mechanical design, embedded controls, and one published paper.

Finishing my BS in Mechanical Engineering at UC Irvine with an Energy Systems focus. Graduating June 2026. I do the usual mechanical stuff — CAD, FEA, drawings — but I'm most useful when the project also needs embedded controls: sensor integration, PID tuning, closed-loop testing.

The work I've done so far includes a 220 N·m electric FSAE drivetrain, a peer-reviewed stormwater biofiltration paper, a WWI airplane fuselage rebuild in SolidWorks, and a senior capstone replacing grid-scale batteries with hybrid CAES and pumped hydro. Different domains, same pattern: analyze, build, test, fix what the analysis missed.

Targeting design engineering roles in renewables, solar, and energy storage. Outside of school: coastal foraging, fishing, and whatever side project I've talked myself into that week.

Selected Work

Things I've built.

Energy Storage PID Control Arduino MATLAB

Hybrid Mechanical Energy Storage System

Senior capstone replacing electrochemical batteries with a hybrid CAES and pumped hydro system on a shared 12V alternator shaft. As Controls Lead, I built the PID voltage regulation on Arduino — holding 12V within 5% through step-change loads — using Hall effect current sensors and voltage dividers for closed-loop feedback.

View Project
SolidWorks ANSYS FEA GD&T DFM

FSAE Electric Powertrain

Designed motor and differential mounts for a 220 N·m electric drivetrain in SolidWorks, validated to >1.5× safety factor through ANSYS stress, torque, and vibratory analysis. Selected a 3.3:1 final drive ratio for slip-limited acceleration within packaging constraints. Applied GD&T for outsourced fabrication, then validated the assembled mounts on a welded test bench.

Sensor Systems R Analytics Field Testing

Mobile Rainfall Simulator & Analytics Pipeline

Engineered a mobile rainfall simulator at SCCWRP that hit a 0.87 uniformity coefficient across soil, pavement, grass, and turf. Built an R pipeline that cut a 30-sensor storm-event setup down to 3 sensors without losing signal fidelity. Co-author on a peer-reviewed paper in ES&T Water (2025).
